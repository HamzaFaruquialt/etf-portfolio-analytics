⌖ Diversification signal

GLD is the only genuine diversifier. It correlates 0.016 with SPY and stays pale against every equity holding, while SPY/QQQ sit at 0.929 — nearly redundant. AGG (bonds) also decouples at 0.075. This is exactly why the min-variance and max-Sharpe solutions lean on gold and bonds rather than stacking correlated equity ETFs.

⌖ Concentration vs diversification

The optimizer makes extreme bets: max-Sharpe goes 67% QQQ / 33% GLD and zeroes out the other six holdings; min-variance parks 93% in AGG. This concentration is precisely why the in-sample Sharpe of 0.89 didn't survive out-of-sample — the optimizer overfit to the lookback window's realized returns.

Tail Risk — VaR & CVaR

Forward-looking loss estimates from 10,000 simulations, run two ways: parametric Monte Carlo (assumes normal) vs historical bootstrap (resamples real days, keeps fat tails).

METHOD.divergence

99% CVaR — normal model vs reality
⌖ Fat tails are real

At 99% confidence, the historical bootstrap CVaR (3.59%) exceeds the parametric estimate (2.23%) by 61% for the equal-weight book. A normal-distribution VaR model would have materially understated worst-case daily loss — a concrete, defensible argument for not trusting a single parametric risk number.
